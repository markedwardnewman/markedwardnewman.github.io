About

 Front End

Stark advocate for W3C’s semantic markup, Google’s Material Design guidelines, WebAIM accessibility standards, and using only those frameworks that are suitable for the task at hand.

Professional contact with backbone.js, tinkering affairs with AngularJS and React, engaged to Angular2. HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript are hand-coded when practical and scaffolded when not. Avoids the chaos by playing with Npm task-runners and build tools while on break.

 Back End

As of early 2017, Apple, Inc. still uses a monitoring program I wrote that has a hand in manufacturing their flagship desktop computer- the vase-shaped Mac Pro. It’s a little known fact that the prototype for the unified thermal core is the actual mouthpiece that once belonged to Darth Vader.

Have developed a couple of from-the-ground-up Ruby on Rails sites with a back-end that shields against nefarious internet shenanigans. Features include SSL encryption, email-based account activation and password reset, encrypted authorizations (using bcrypt), session-based cookies, and regexually constrained form validation.

I’ve a Google Play distributed Android app that’s unapologetically enhanced with their Adwords API. Its 500+ user-base now generate enough revenue to buy me at least one extra fancy coffee a week.

Enough Drupal experience to be dangerous. This includes set-up and deployment, user account administration, theme and template creation, server-side code integration, and all things front-end. Recent proponent of Kirby, a database-less, file-based CMS.

 Project Mgmt.

Was a project manager in the construction industry for 5 years before I finally getting my groove on. Am intimately aware of large-scale project lifecycles, from start to finish. Tuning short-term business objectives to long-term strategic goals is a meta-skill I keep in my back pocket. Huge wads of cash could (maybe) persuade me to switch gears from front-end development to project management.
